Knowing Your Rights

In 2020, The State of NY repealed law 50-A, legislation which protected NYPD disciplinary records from public access. The move was decided in the wake of widespread protests due to the murder of George Floyd at the hands of police, and sought to provide better accountability for police misconduct.




Power to the People

Between 1999 and 2020, NYC residents filed 192,466 complaints against NYPD officers via the Civilian Complaint Review Board.

Over the past five years, a majority of complaints received by the CCRB involved either abuse of authority or force. At the beginning of this period, police-worn body cameras also became more prevalent, adding extra work to the review process, especially in cases of alleged force or abuse of authority.




Prior to the repeal of 50-A, the State of NY had already released its fiscal plan for the CCRB. From 2020 to 2021, the budget remained the same, despite a high volume of open reviews within the agency. In fact, the CCRB only releases data oonce reports are processed, which could likely mean the data from the past few years is far from complete.
